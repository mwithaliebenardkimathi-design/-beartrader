Home

Welcome to Bearghosts Bears—simple, copy-ready guidance for forex traders who value confirmation, patience, and risk control.

  • Mission: Trade with discipline, document with honesty, and teach with clarity.
  • Core rule: Never enter a trade without confirmation—waiting can save you from losses.
  • Focus pairs: EURUSD, GBPUSD, XAUUSD, USDJPY.

Strategy

A step-by-step approach you can copy, share, and refine.

  1. Market bias: Identify trend on the higher timeframe (H4/D1) using structure highs and lows.
  2. Key levels: Mark supply and demand, support and resistance, and session highs and lows.
  3. Confirmation: Wait for a break and retest or a clear rejection candle at your level.
  4. Entry: Use limit or stop orders only after confirmation; avoid impulsive market entries.
  5. Management: Move to breakeven after price reaches 1R; scale out at key levels.

Tip: If confirmation is weak, skip the trade—capital preservation beats participation.

Risk

Keep risk small and consistent—your edge lives in repetition, not hero trades.

  • Risk per trade: 1% or less of account balance.
  • R-multiple target: Aim for 1.5R to 3R depending on structure and volatility.
  • Daily cap: Stop trading after 2 consecutive losses or a 3% drawdown.
  • News filter: Avoid entries 15 minutes before or after high-impact news.

Reminder: Waiting for confirmation reduces drawdowns and emotional decisions.

Education

Short, practical lessons you can teach and reuse.

Market structure

Higher highs and higher lows signal uptrends; lower highs and lower lows signal downtrends. Trade with the dominant leg.

Liquidity

Price often grabs liquidity above or below obvious highs and lows—wait for the sweep, then look for confirmation.

Sessions

London provides direction; New York provides continuation or reversal. Define your session and stick to it.

Candles

Rejection wicks at key levels matter only with context—pair them with structure and timing.

Journal

Document every trade—setup, confirmation, risk, result, and lesson.

Template

  • Pair and direction: e.g., EURUSD long
  • Higher timeframe bias: Uptrend or Downtrend
  • Level: Supply or Demand, Support or Resistance
  • Confirmation: Break and retest, Rejection candle, Liquidity sweep
  • Entry and stop loss: Price and stop size in pips
  • Risk: Percent per trade
  • Outcome: R-multiple and notes
  • Lesson: What to repeat and what to avoid

Rule: If a trade is not journaled, it did not happen—no data, no improvement.
